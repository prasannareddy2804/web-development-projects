About Us

Welcome to Wildlife Care and Rescue, where we are dedicated to the protection and rehabilitation of wildlife. Our organization was founded with the goal of providing a safe haven for injured, orphaned, and displaced animals.

At Wildlife Care and Rescue, our team of experienced and passionate professionals works tirelessly to make a positive impact on the lives of wild animals. Whether you are interested in volunteering, donating, or simply learning more about wildlife conservation, there are many ways you can get involved and support our cause.

Our Departments

1. Conservation Department

Our Conservation Department is dedicated to the long-term well-being of wildlife and their habitats. We actively engage in conservation initiatives, research, and advocacy to address the root causes of threats to biodiversity. Our work involves habitat restoration, wildlife monitoring, and collaboration with governmental and non-governmental partners to implement sustainable conservation practices.

2. Research Department

The Research Department at Wildlife Care and Rescue focuses on scientific inquiry to better understand wildlife behavior, ecology, and health. Our researchers collaborate with academic institutions and contribute valuable data to support evidence-based conservation strategies.

3. Wildlife Rehabilitation Department

Our Wildlife Rehabilitation Department provides specialized care for injured, orphaned, and traumatized animals. The dedicated team of rehabilitators ensures that each animal receives appropriate medical treatment and rehabilitation support tailored to its specific needs.

4. Ecotourism Department

The Ecotourism Department at Wildlife Care and Rescue promotes responsible and sustainable tourism practices. We offer educational and immersive experiences that allow visitors to connect with nature while contributing to the organization's conservation efforts. All proceeds from ecotourism activities support our wildlife care and rescue initiatives.

Our work

Protecting Endangered Species

Wildlife conservation involves protecting and preserving the habitats of endangered species. By focusing on the conservation of these species, we contribute to maintaining biodiversity and the balance of ecosystems.

Habitat Restoration

Efforts are made to restore and rehabilitate damaged or degraded habitats. This includes reforestation, wetland restoration, and other activities that help recreate a suitable environment for various wildlife species.

Community Engagement

Wildlife conservation often involves working closely with local communities. Engaging communities in conservation efforts helps raise awareness, build support, and create sustainable solutions that benefit both people and wildlife.
